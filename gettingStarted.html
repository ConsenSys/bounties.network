Getting Started

What is the Bounties Network?

The Bounties Network makes it easy to outsource any task, completed by top talent from anywhere in the world. Some examples of tasks we’ve seen so far are: code, marketing projects, social impact and everything in between. All payments are safe & secure thanks to Ethereum, and freelancers get paid as soon as the work they submit gets accepted. Put up bounties paying in any token, 100% fee free.

Why the Bounties Bee?

Bees are the bounty hunters of the natural world. They fly from flower to flower, gathering nectar for themselves or their hive to thrive on. The Bounties Network is about helping humans get things done dynamically, in an organic and empowering way.

Why does MetaMask lock up?

Access to the Bounties Network will get locked by MetaMask if you have not used the network for a certain period of time. This happens because MetaMask automatically locks your account if unattended for some time. To unlock, simply click on the MetaMask extension and type in your password.

Getting Ether (ETH)

For U.S. citizens only: you can buy ether (ETH) in MetaMask. ETH is a digital currency that enables you to use Bounties Network and many other distributed applications on Ethereum.

For everyone else: you can purchase ETH from an exchange (e.g. Coinbase) through the MetaMask link. Right now, you will need to use your MetaMask wallet to interact with the Bounties Network, and cannot do it directly from an exchange.

How does the bounty process work?

The process starts with the creation of a bounty. To create a bounty and ensure clarity and transparency, the bounty issuer is guided through a series of questions about the task/project they are creating. A clear description, expected deliverables, tokens staked and all key requirements laid out from the start ensure successful submissions.

Once the bounty is created, it gets added to the pool of open bounties within the network. Task fulfillers can then browse open bounties based on their category of interest and specific skill set. Once they find a project they’d like to complete, they submit their intention to work on the bounty.

When the work is completed, task fulfillers submit the relevant deliverable files (or links to those deliverables) for review by the issuer. Task issuers are able to comment on and rate submissions. Multiple iterations may be needed to achieve the desired result so feedback is key.

Once the task issuer is satisfied and accepts a given submission, the relevant tokens are immediately transferred to the individual who made the submission. The on-chain acceptance triggers the transfer of all relevant intellectual property which was submitted as part of the deliverable.

When the transaction is finalised, both the issuer and the fulfiller can publicly rate and comment on each other and ensure the network is always defined by transparency and fairness.

I want to issue a bounty, what’s in it for me?

  • No fees: 100% of the money for the bounty goes to the fulfiller.
  • Top talent from across the globe: The Bounties Network connects you with talented people from all over the world. You can use bounties to scout full-time talent or freelance work for immediate needs.
  • Clear task creation: We provide a step-by-step bounty creation guide to help you define clear tasks and expectations.

I want to work on bounties, what’s in it for me?

  • Find work: Find bounties that match your skills quickly and easily, and get paid a rate you deserve for your work.
  • Build your business: The Bounties Network helps you grow and manage your freelancing business and at your own pace, in your own time with steady flow of great quality projects.
  • Build a reputation: because the data on the Bounties Network is unchangeable, you can build a stellar record of the work and positive recommendations without worrying about scams.
  • Is there a minimum amount of ETH I need: Right now at a gas price of 4Gwei, it only takes ~0.000738ETH to fulfill a given bounty so roughly ~$0.75 USD. This serves as a bit of a bond for the fulfiller (i.e. you’re not just a spammer, you truly believe your submission is correct)
